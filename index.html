Who Is This For?

🔍 Crypto Beginners Who Want to Understand Their Own Wallet

You bought some ETH, made a few transactions, and now your wallet history looks like a wall of hexadecimal gibberish. cryptoucan.xyz translates all of that into plain English. See exactly what you've done on-chain — what you swapped, what you received, how long you've held — without needing to understand how the blockchain actually works under the hood.

It's the fastest way to get a clear picture of your own wallet without touching a block explorer.

🧐 Researchers and Analysts Investigating Wallets

Whether you're tracing funds, studying on-chain behaviour, or doing due diligence on a counterparty, cryptoucan.xyz gives you an instant readable summary of any public Ethereum address. See wallet age, activity patterns, notable transactions, and token holdings at a glance — without parsing raw data manually.

Use the built-in AI prompt to feed the wallet summary directly into ChatGPT or Claude for a deeper structured analysis in seconds.

💼 Investors Checking a Wallet Before Sending Funds

Before you send ETH or tokens to an address — whether it's a project wallet, a new exchange, or someone you're dealing with — it pays to know what that wallet has done. Is it newly created? Has it ever interacted with major protocols? How active is it? cryptoucan.xyz answers these questions instantly, giving you a basic sanity check before any transfer.

All Ethereum wallet data is public. You're entitled to look — and knowing is always better than guessing.

🏛️ DeFi Users Reviewing Their Activity

If you use DeFi protocols — Uniswap, Aave, Lido, Curve, and others — your wallet history can get complex fast. cryptoucan.xyz recognises interactions with major protocols and describes them in plain English: "Swapped tokens on Uniswap", "Staked ETH on Lido", "Deposited into Aave". It's a fast way to review what your wallet has been doing without deep-diving into Etherscan every time.

🎓 Students and Developers Learning About Ethereum

cryptoucan.xyz is a great learning tool for anyone studying how Ethereum works in practice. Look up famous wallets — Vitalik's address, major exchange hot wallets, early ETH holders — and see their transaction history in human-readable form. Understanding real on-chain behaviour is one of the best ways to learn how the Ethereum ecosystem actually operates.
